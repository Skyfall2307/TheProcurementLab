Cost Drivers in Procurement: Why Price Is Only the Beginning?

April 29, 2025 • By Katarzyna Fonteyn

In mature procurement organizations, price is no longer the main point. It is simply the starting point. True procurement leadership comes from a deeper capability: understanding the forces that drive cost.

At the heart of this capability is the use of raw material indexes. These indexes are far more than passive market data; they are strategic levers that, when properly applied, transform how procurement professionals manage categories, suppliers, and long-term costs.

Raw Material Indexes as Strategic Tools

When integrated with cost driver analysis, raw material indexes reveal the complete story behind supplier pricing and market trends. They allow procurement teams to move beyond surface-level negotiations and into a proactive understanding of how inputs like metals, resins, freight, and energy shape the costs of goods and services.

Examples:

  • Aluminum component sourcing → Track London Metal Exchange (LME) aluminum index.
  • Flexible packaging → Monitor resin prices like polyethylene or polypropylene.
  • Freight → Follow indexes like trucking rates for transport costs.

This integration is critical to building sophisticated Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) models, allowing proactive cost management rather than reactive price responses.

Cost Drivers Beyond Manufacturing: Corporate Travel

Cost driver analysis is equally important in service categories like travel procurement. Early in my career, I applied cost driver thinking to travel management:

For air travel:

  • Fuel surcharges (linked to global jet fuel indexes)
  • Airport fees and taxes
  • Load factors (flight occupancy rates)
  • Seasonality patterns (summer, events, etc...)

For hotels:

  • Occupancy rates driving seasonal increases
  • Labor cost impacts on hotel operations
  • Energy costs influencing room rates

By breaking down these drivers, we negotiated stronger agreements and predicted market shifts more accurately.

From Risk Management to Competitive Advantage

Procurement teams with deep cost driver understanding unlock key advantages:

  • Structured Buying: Timing purchases when raw material costs are favorable.
  • Index-Linked Contracts: Pricing formulas based on public indexes for transparency.
  • Supplier Collaboration: Shifting negotiations to focus on underlying input costs, not surface price arguments.

Ultimately, controlling cost drivers provides a more strategic and powerful position than reacting to surface-level price movements.

Learning from Experience

At companies like UPM, I learned early how to embed raw material indexes and cost driver analysis into procurement strategies. Real procurement excellence comes not just from tracking costs, but from understanding the forces behind them — and shaping them proactively.

The Leadership Imperative

For procurement professionals who aspire to lead: mastering cost drivers is no longer optional. It is a core capability that transforms tactical buyers into strategic leaders.

Don't just monitor costs. Understand them — and shape them.
