Where to Find Information for 2025 about S2P

April 27, 2025 • By Katarzyna Fonteyn

Staying on top of Source-to-Pay (S2P) and Accounts Payable (AP) technology trends is more important than ever in today's rapidly evolving procurement landscape.

Whether you are new to procurement, entering the SaaS procurement technology space, or simply trying to make informed vendor selections, it can be overwhelming to navigate the numerous platforms and providers available. I’ve experienced this first-hand, and I know that accessing reliable, up-to-date information makes all the difference.

To help you cut through the noise, I have compiled a list of trusted resources and reports that will guide your exploration and understanding of the procurement and AP technology ecosystem in 2025.

1. Gartner Magic Quadrant for Source-to-Pay Suites (2025)

Gartner’s Magic Quadrant evaluates leading S2P technology providers based on their ability to execute and completeness of vision. It is a valuable visual guide to assess which vendors are leaders, challengers, visionaries, or niche players in the market.

Vendors typically featured: Coupa, Ivalua, SAP Ariba, Jaggaer, and GEP Smart.

2. Gartner Magic Quadrant for Accounts Payable Invoice Automation (2025)

Recognizing the growing importance of AP automation, Gartner now publishes a dedicated Magic Quadrant for Accounts Payable Invoice Automation (APIA) solutions. This helps procurement and finance teams identify best-in-class platforms for automating invoice capture, validation, and payment workflows.

Leading APIA vendors often include: Basware, Medius, Tungsten Network, and Tipalti.

3. Gartner Peer Insights: Source-to-Pay Suites

Gartner Peer Insights offers user-driven ratings and reviews from real procurement practitioners. It complements analyst reports by providing practical feedback about implementation experiences, customer service, product usability, and overall satisfaction with S2P solutions.

This is an excellent resource to validate vendor marketing claims against real-world client outcomes.

4. Ardent Partners Research: The State of Source-to-Pay Digitization 2024

Ardent Partners’ research captures insights from hundreds of procurement executives, focusing on digitization progress, pain points, and opportunities across the S2P lifecycle. The 2024 report in particular explores the increasing use of automation, analytics, and artificial intelligence within procurement organizations.

It provides benchmarks for how digitally mature organizations are and how procurement can evolve to drive greater business value.

5. 2025 Supplier Management Technology Advisor

This guide helps procurement leaders evaluate Supplier Information Management (SIM), Supplier Risk Management (SRM), and Supplier Performance Management (SPM) solutions. Strong supplier management platforms enable better risk mitigation, compliance, and relationship development across global supply chains.

Examples of solution providers in this space include: HICX, SynerTrade, and SAP Fieldglass.

6. The Hackett Group Inc. Insights: 2025 CPO Agenda – Gen AI Takes Center Stage

The Hackett Group’s 2025 CPO Agenda examines how Chief Procurement Officers are prioritizing investment in generative AI and automation to manage rising workloads and expectations. The report outlines the transformative potential of Gen AI across spend analysis, sourcing, supplier engagement, and contract management.

Hackett also highlights skills procurement teams must develop to capitalize on AI technologies while maintaining strategic relevance.

7. Spend Matters SolutionMap: Spring 2025 Rankings

Spend Matters SolutionMap provides quarterly benchmarking of procurement technology vendors across different categories such as S2P, Procure-to-Pay (P2P), Contingent Workforce, and Supplier Management. Their analysis combines analyst scoring with actual customer input to generate credible comparative rankings.

Spend Matters groups vendors by company size needs, helping organizations choose solutions based on specific priorities whether for large enterprises, mid-market companies, or specialist use cases.
