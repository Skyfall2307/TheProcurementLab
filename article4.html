Sustainable Procurement is Your Strategic Imperative

26th of April 2025 • By Katarzyna Fonteyn

The Expanding Impact of Supply Chains

Organisations’ environmental and social footprints extend far beyond their direct operations. Emissions and impacts are typically classified into three categories:

  • Scope 1: Direct emissions from owned facilities and vehicles.
  • Scope 2: Indirect emissions from purchased electricity, heating, and cooling.
  • Scope 3: Indirect emissions from the value chain, including suppliers and customers.

In many industries, Scope 3 emissions account for over two-thirds of the total footprint. Procurement has evolved into a strategic function influencing the environmental and social outcomes of the entire business ecosystem.

Rethinking Procurement for a Sustainable Future

Sustainable procurement means embedding sustainability into every purchasing decision. Leading organisations now:

  • Integrate environmental, social, and economic criteria from supplier selection through contract management.
  • Foster long-term partnerships with aligned suppliers.
  • Prioritise transparency, accountability, and shared responsibility across the supply chain.

Procurement teams today are not just negotiators — they are builders of a more sustainable, innovative, and equitable future.

Key Drivers for Sustainable Procurement

Environmental Pressures

Procurement decisions influence:

  • Climate change mitigation
  • Nature protection
  • Resource conservation

Sourcing from circular or low-carbon suppliers is critical for meeting corporate goals.

Social Expectations

Businesses must ensure ethical practices across supplier tiers, including:

  • Labour rights and safe conditions
  • Banning child labour and forced labour
  • Equity and diverse supplier support
  • Community development

Regulatory Shifts

Companies face stricter regulations around:

  • Due diligence on human rights and environment
  • Disclosure of supply chain practices
  • Penalties for environmental and social harm

Business Value and Innovation

Sustainable procurement drives resilience, cost savings, and innovation by:

  • Building resilient supply chains
  • Driving efficiency through circular sourcing models
  • Stimulating innovation with low-carbon technologies and ethical practices
  • Enhancing brand reputation and market access

The Road Ahead: Transforming Procurement Functions

Procurement teams must:

  • Shift to strategic supplier partnerships
  • Develop deep multi-tier supply chain visibility
  • Embed environmental and social KPIs into contracts
  • Invest in supplier development and circular economy models

The future belongs to those who make sustainable procurement a competitive advantage.
